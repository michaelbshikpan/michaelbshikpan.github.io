PhD Student in Linguistics · University of Toronto

Michael Bulkaam Shikpan

I am a PhD student in Linguistics at the University of Toronto, working on language documentation, phonetics, tonal phonology, morphotonology, and the phonology–morphosyntax interface. My research focuses on under-documented Nigerian heritage languages, especially Chadic and selected Niger-Congo languages. Since 2015, I have been involved in primary fieldwork and language documentation across more than 20 under-documented languages, with a commitment to developing dictionaries, orthographies, pedagogical grammars, and community-centered resources for African language documentation, revitalization, and linguistic research. Through my research and practical work, I aim to create resources that preserve linguistic diversity and empower communities to maintain, develop, and transmit their heritage languages as part of cultural identity and societal development.

Research

Research interests

My research brings together primary fieldwork, language documentation, phonological analysis, and community-based language development. I am especially interested in how tone and vowel harmony interact with morphology and morphosyntax in Tal and related West Chadic languages.
